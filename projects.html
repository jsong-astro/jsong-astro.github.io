Research Projects

Ongoing and completed research in galaxy science

 CSST Galaxy Science Research Project

The China Space Station Telescope (CSST) is a next-generation space survey telescope that will conduct large-area multiband imaging and slitless spectroscopic surveys. As part of the CSST galaxy science working group, I contribute to multiple aspects of the preparatory science program.

  • Galaxy Nonparametric Morphology Measurement Software

    Development of statmorph_csst, an updated framework for measuring nonparametric morphology indicators (CAS, Gini-M20, MID, etc.) optimized for CSST imaging data. This tool extends the capabilities of existing packages to handle the specific characteristics of CSST observations, including its PSF properties and noise characteristics.

  • Galaxy Morphology Classification Using Machine Learning

    Development of USmorph, an updated framework for automatic galaxy morphology classification. USmorph integrates rotationally-invariant supervised machine learning methods with unsupervised learning data sets, providing robust and efficient morphological classification for large survey data sets. The framework has been applied to galaxies in the COSMOS field and published in ApJS (Song et al. 2024).

  • Environmental Effects on Galaxy Properties at High Redshift

    Analysis of how the local and large-scale environment influences the physical properties of galaxies at high redshift (0.5 < z < 2.5). Using data from the COSMOS-DASH field, this work investigates the environmental dependence of stellar mass, star formation rate, and morphology for the most massive galaxies (Song et al. 2023).

 CSST IFS Instrument Performance Evaluation

The CSST Integral Field Spectrograph (IFS) will provide spatially-resolved spectroscopy for a wide range of astrophysical targets. I am involved in evaluating the scientific performance of this instrument through simulations and pipeline development.

  • CSST IFS Data Analysis Pipeline Development

    Building and testing a comprehensive data reduction and analysis pipeline for the CSST IFS. The pipeline handles the full workflow from raw data processing to scientific measurements, including spectral extraction, flux calibration, and emission line fitting.

  • Recovery of Galaxy Star Formation Histories

    Evaluating the capability of the CSST IFS to recover galaxy star formation histories (SFHs) through spectral energy distribution fitting of simulated IFS datacubes. This work assesses how well spatially-resolved SFHs can be constrained given the instrument's spectral resolution, wavelength coverage, and expected signal-to-noise ratios.
